# Analyst handoff

The dataset contains substantial skew in ownership, review activity, and playtime-related variables, particularly among highly popular multiplayer titles.

Some early exploratory summaries suggested that free-to-play games exhibited more variable user sentiment overall, with indications of weaker average ratings in certain segments.

There were also tentative observations that premium-priced titles may show longer engagement patterns, although the relationship appeared inconsistent across genres.

A portion of the extreme playtime observations was suspected to reflect idle-time inflation or non-representative activity patterns rather than typical engagement behavior.

Prior reporting occasionally treated review volume as a rough proxy for both commercial success and perceived game quality, though these relationships were not rigorously validated.
